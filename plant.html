Medicinal Plants Database

Comprehensive guide to 12 key medicinal plants of Northern Thailand, their traditional uses, and benefits for senior health.

Turmeric (Curcuma longa)

Chiang Mai 0 km Year-round

Plant photo from Unsplash/Pexels

Traditional Uses

  • Anti-inflammatory for arthritis and joint pain
  • Liver detoxification and digestive support
  • Wound healing and skin conditions
  • Natural preservative in food preparation

Senior Health Benefits

Reduces chronic joint inflammation, supports liver function in medication metabolism, and provides antioxidant protection against age-related cellular damage.

Scientific Research

Curcumin, the active compound in turmeric, has been extensively studied for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Research from Chiang Mai University confirms its effectiveness in managing osteoarthritis in elderly populations.

Gotu Kola (Centella asiatica)

Chiang Mai 0 km Rainy season (Jun-Oct)

Plant photo from Unsplash/Pexels

Traditional Uses

  • Cognitive enhancement and memory improvement
  • Wound healing and scar reduction
  • Treatment of venous insufficiency
  • Anxiety and stress relief

Senior Health Benefits

Improves cerebral circulation for better memory retention, accelerates healing of pressure ulcers and diabetic wounds, and supports vascular health in aging populations.

Scientific Research

Studies from the Faculty of Pharmacy at Chiang Mai University demonstrate that Gotu Kola's triterpenoids significantly improve cognitive function in elderly patients with mild cognitive impairment.

Moringa (Moringa oleifera)

Chiang Mai 0 km Dry season (Nov-May)

Plant photo from Unsplash/Pexels

Traditional Uses

  • Nutritional supplementation for malnutrition
  • Blood sugar regulation for diabetes
  • Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant support
  • Immune system enhancement

Senior Health Benefits

Addresses age-related nutritional deficiencies, helps maintain healthy blood glucose levels, and provides comprehensive antioxidant protection against chronic diseases.

Scientific Research

Research published in the Journal of Ethnopharmacology confirms Moringa's effectiveness in managing type 2 diabetes and improving nutritional status in elderly populations.

Butterfly Pea (Clitoria ternatea)

Lamphun 24 km Year-round

Plant photo from Unsplash/Pexels

Traditional Uses

  • Cognitive enhancement and memory support
  • Natural food coloring and antioxidant
  • Stress relief and mood enhancement
  • Eye health and vision support

Senior Health Benefits

Enhances cognitive function through anthocyanin compounds, provides natural stress relief without sedation, and supports retinal health for age-related vision changes.

Greater Galangal (Alpinia galanga)

Lampang 76 km Rainy season (Jun-Oct)

Plant photo from Unsplash/Pexels

Traditional Uses

  • Digestive aid for nausea and bloating
  • Immune support during cold season
  • Anti-microbial properties for infections
  • Culinary spice in traditional dishes

Senior Health Benefits

Addresses common digestive issues in seniors taking multiple medications, boosts immune function during seasonal changes, and provides natural antimicrobial support.
