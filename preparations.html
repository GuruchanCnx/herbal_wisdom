Traditional Preparation Methods

Step-by-step guides for preparing herbal remedies safely and effectively for senior wellness.

Daily Supplements & Herbal Teas

Butterfly Pea Tea

Butterfly Pea Tea

Preparation:

  1. Use 5-7 fresh or dried flowers
  2. Steep in hot water (80Â°C) for 5-10 minutes
  3. Strain and drink 1-2 cups daily

Benefits:

Rich in anthocyanins that cross the blood-brain barrier, enhancing memory and reducing oxidative stress.

Cautions:

May cause mild drowsiness. Avoid excessive consumption.

Gotu Kola Tea

Gotu Kola Tea

Preparation:

  1. Use 1-2 teaspoons of dried leaves
  2. Steep in hot water for 10-15 minutes
  3. Drink 1 cup daily with meals

Benefits:

Contains triterpenoids that stimulate collagen production and improve cerebral blood flow.

Cautions:

May interact with sedatives and diabetes medications.

Topical Applications & External Use

Turmeric Paste

Turmeric Healing Paste

Preparation:

  1. Mix 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
  2. Add enough water or coconut oil to form paste
  3. Apply to affected areas 1-2 times daily

Benefits:

Curcumin provides potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Cautions:

May stain skin temporarily. Perform patch test first.

Siam Weed Poultice

Siam Weed Emergency Poultice

Preparation:

  1. Crush fresh leaves into a paste
  2. Apply directly to fresh wounds immediately
  3. Reapply 2-3 times daily until healed

Benefits:

Contains chromomoric acid that promotes rapid blood clotting and tissue regeneration.

Cautions:

Ensure proper wound cleaning before application.

Dietary Integration & Culinary Use

Moringa Smoothie

Moringa Nutritional Smoothie

Preparation:

  1. Add fresh leaves to smoothies
  2. Or mix 1-2 teaspoons powder into beverages
  3. Consume daily with meals

Benefits:

Exceptionally rich in vitamins A, C, calcium, and potassium.

Cautions:

High fiber content may cause digestive discomfort if introduced too quickly.

Greater Galangal Curry

Greater Galangal Digestive Curry

Preparation:

  1. Slice 1-2 inches of fresh rhizome
  2. Add to soups, curries, or stir-fries
  3. Consume 2-3 times per week

Benefits:

Galangin compounds stimulate digestive enzyme production and have antimicrobial properties.

Cautions:

May interact with blood thinners. Use cautiously if taking anticoagulants.
